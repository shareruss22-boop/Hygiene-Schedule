Gypsy & Me
The Roadmap

Foundation — Horse & Self
0/7
Get Gypsy healthy — treat ulcers, resolve lamenessPriority
Everything starts here. A healthy horse is a therapy horse.
Complete ownership transfer and get insurance
You need to own her before any formal program involvement
Establish consistent groundwork routineFoundation
Daily handling, leading, desensitization — this IS therapy horse training
Research PATH InternationalFree
pathintl.org — premier equine therapy org. Read their horse standards and requirements
Get CPR and first aid certified~$50–100
Required for most programs. Red Cross offers courses regularly
Read foundational books on equine assisted learningLibrary
"Horses Don't Lie" by Chris Irwin · "The Tao of Equus" by Linda Kohanov
Join online equine therapy communitiesFree
Facebook groups, Reddit r/equinetherapy — learn from practitioners already doing this work
2
Month 6 — Month 12
Education & Training
0/7
Complete an Equine Assisted Learning intro course~$200–500
EAGALA and OK Corral Series both offer online intro courses
Volunteer at a local therapeutic riding centerFree
Invaluable hands-on experience. Search PATH centers near Kissimmee
Complete Gypsy's temperament evaluationKey Step
Most programs require a formal horse temperament assessment
Study natural horsemanship principlesFree–Low Cost
Parelli, Clinton Anderson YouTube — groundwork skills translate directly to therapy work
Research liability insurance for equine activities~$300–500/yr
Separate from horse insurance. Needed if people interact with Gypsy in any structured way
Connect with a licensed mental health professionalPartnership
EAP requires a licensed professional partner. EAL does not — but a partnership opens more doors
Document Gypsy's training progress and temperamentFree
Keep a journal of her responses to different people, environments and situations
3
Month 12 — Month 18
Building Your Practice
0/6
Complete EAGALA foundational training~$1,000–1,500
The gold standard EAL/EAP certification. Can be done without a license for the equine specialist role
Shadow experienced equine therapy practitionersFree
Ask PATH centers or EAGALA practitioners if you can observe sessions
Design simple groundwork based activitiesCreative
Develop your own session activities using Gypsy's strengths and personality
Consult a lawyer about liability and business structure~$100–300
Even informal sessions need liability protection. An LLC may make sense
Identify your target populationFocus
Children, veterans, anxiety/trauma — Gypsy's calm energy suits anxiety and trauma work beautifully
Attend an equine therapy conference or workshop~$200–500
EAGALA annual conference, PATH Intl symposium — incredible networking and learning
4
Month 18 — Year 2+
Launching & Growing
0/6
Partner with an existing therapy center or wellness practiceCollaboration
Rather than starting from scratch, bring Gypsy to an established program
Formalize liability waivers and participant agreements~$200–400
Non-negotiable before anyone interacts with Gypsy in a structured program context
Run pilot sessions with trusted friends or familyFree
Low stakes practice runs. Observe how Gypsy responds to different people and energy
Pursue continuing education annuallyOngoing
The field evolves constantly. Annual workshops keep your skills current
Document and evaluate your sessionsFree
Keep records of activities, participant responses and Gypsy's behavior
Explore nonprofit or grant funding optionsFree to research
Equine therapy programs qualify for various grants — could offset Gypsy's costs long term
